Melbourne is a city of 5,000,000 people in the south-east of Australia. It is often voted the world's most liveable city.

Things to do in Melbourne

  • Central Melbourne has many lovely gardens to enjoy, including the Royal Botanic Gardens and Fitzroy Gardens.
  • St Kilda has beaches and many restaurants, particularly around Acland St. From the city, take the 96 tram to stop #136. Tram 16 also goes there (and is the better option). Alternatively, take tram 3/3a to St Kilda junction and walk down Fitzroy St.
  • Penguins at St Kilda: At sunset, clusters of little penguins come ashore on the St. Kilda breakwater and waddle up to their nests in the rocks around 8.30pm every evening.
  • National Gallery of Victoria.
  • Fairfield Boathouse: enjoy lunch and a paddle on the Yarra River.
  • Escape Room Melbourne: You have 70 minutes to unravel the mystery of a hidden room, working as a team to solve quirky and inventive puzzles to escape.
  • Food Tours: Queenies or Food experiences
  • Kite boarding, Wind surfing, and Paddle boarding lessons
  • Queen Victoria Market: an iconic Melbourne market (the deli and meat and fish halls are best)
  • Go sailing on the Enterprize, an historic tall ship.
  • A highlight of the world's cricket calendar: Australia vs England in the Boxing Day Test, Melbourne Cricket Ground, December 26-30, 2017.

Things to do outside Melbourne

  • Healesville sanctuary: Healesville sanctuary has many iconic Australian animals to see and interact with, including koalas, platypus, reptiles and birds. It is a 1 hour drive north east from Clayton campus. This the venue for one of the conference excursions.
  • Upper Yarra Valley: The Upper Yarra Valley is a lush region with many vineyards.
  • The Great Ocean Road is one of the world's most scenic coastal drives, winding past excellent surfing beaches. Can be done in a day, but best as an overnight trip.
  • The Mornington Peninsula is a beautiful area south-east of Melbourne with vineyards, beaches, and seaside towns.
  • Penguin Parade: Phillip Island is home to a large fairy penguin colony. The penguins can be viewed returning home at sunset every day (for a fee).
