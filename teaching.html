Undergraduate Teaching

I teach MTH3170 - Network Mathematics and MTH3175 - Network Mathematics (Advanced). These units are introductions to graph theory focusing on proofs and algorithms. The two units share the same content and the same lectures. The tutorials and assessment are different. MTH3170 emphasises algorithms (while not neglecting proofs), while MTH3175 emphasises proofs (while not neglecting algorithms). MTH3175 is more for students doing a major in mathematics, especially for those aiming to do honours in mathematics. MTH3170 is for all mathematically capable students, including those doing degrees in Science, Education, or Computer Science. If you are a Monash student interested in mathematics and computer science, then I recommend you take these units.
